RLCH: Safe RL Navigation Based on Action Chunking and Homology CLass

Anonymous authors

Abstract

Collision risk perception is a critical challenge in reinforcement learning -based robot navigation, directly impacting system safety and practicality.

To solve this, this chapter proposes a collision risk-aware RL navigation method (RLCH), focusing on improving the Critic network’s collision risk perception accuracy and recognition ability. Its main innovations are twofold: 1) A homology-based difference reward function for online training quantifies the homology discrepancy between reference and model-generated trajectories, resolving the inherent conflict between goal-approaching and obstacle-avoidance rewards. 2) A negative sampling strategy for offline training collects collision-related trajectory samples, enabling the Critic network to perceive collision penalty signals and enhance its collision risk sensitivity.

Experiments on private and public datasets show that the proposed reward mechanism outperforms mainstream ones in key metrics (navigation safety, motion efficiency, zero-shot generalization), providing technical support for RL’s practical application in robot safe navigation.
